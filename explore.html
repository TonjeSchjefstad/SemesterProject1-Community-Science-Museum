Explore the Community Science Museum

At the Community Science Museum, we believe that science is for everyone. Whether you're a young learner, a teacher looking to inspire your students, or a researcher seeking to advance your work, we have something for you.

Dive into the world of discovery and explore the wonders of science, nature, and technology!

For the Kids

Are you a young person looking to learn more about science? Come on down to our museum, there’s plenty to see and do. You can learn about Newtonian physics from our bumper swing, or why not travel back in time and meet our resident Woolly Mammoth? Our exhibits are designed to be accessible for interested minds, so make sure you come ready to learn and explore.

Young Stars Club

The Young Stars Club is a fun and interactive program for curious kids who love science! Every Saturday from 10:00 AM to 1:00 PM, join our expert team for hands-on experiments, exciting activities, and cool science projects. Whether you’re building a rocket, exploring biology, or experimenting with physics, there’s always something new to discover.

Come ready to learn, explore, and make new friends in a fun, science-filled environment. See you there!

Holiday Club

The Holiday Club is a special program designed for kids during school breaks! Each holiday, we pick a new exciting theme—like space exploration, dinosaurs, or robotics—and dive into hands-on activities, experiments, and fun learning experiences. It’s the perfect way to keep curious minds engaged and make new friends while exploring the wonders of science.

Spaces are limited, so be sure to sign up and join us for a holiday adventure you won’t forget!

For Teachers

As a community-driven museum, we want to work with schools to create places of learning and exploring. Our team are on hand to give your students guided tours of the museum, teach them in our learning laboratory, and provide great video presentations that will excite and inspire them.

Please get in touch with us here to find out more about our facilities and to arrange a time to bring your students through for a visit.

For Researchers
