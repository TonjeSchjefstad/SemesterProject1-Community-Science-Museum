Special Events and Exhibitions

At the Community Science Museum, we’re always excited to offer new and engaging experiences for our visitors. Our special events and exhibitions provide unique opportunities to explore cutting-edge topics, interact with experts in the field, and immerse yourself in the wonders of science. Whether you're interested in aeronautics, enjoying a family-friendly sleepover, or experiencing the latest traveling exhibition, there’s something for everyone to enjoy.

Here's a look at what’s coming up:

Visiting Professor of Aeronautics

We are thrilled to announce that Professor Sheila Widnall from the prestigious Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) will be joining us for a series of three exciting lectures on the development of aeronautics and the future of this ever-evolving field. As one of the leading experts in aeronautics and aerospace engineering, Prof. Widnall will take you through the history, challenges, and cutting-edge innovations that are shaping the future of flight.

Lecture Date: 8. January 2025

Location: Community Science Museum Auditorium

Admission: Free

Join us for this rare opportunity to learn from a trailblazer in the world of aeronautics, and discover where the skies are taking us!

Night in the Museum

Have you ever wanted to sleep beside dinosaurs or under the shadow of scientific discoveries? Now’s your chance! Gather your family for an unforgettable night at the museum. Our Night in the Museum event lets you sleep over in the heart of the museum, surrounded by fascinating exhibits and interactive displays.

Bring your own sleeping bag and prepare for a night full of exploration, games, and educational activities. From discovering the secrets of ancient fossils to exploring the wonders of modern science, this is a night your family won’t forget.

Date: 15. January 2025

Time: 18:00 - 8:00 AM

Don’t miss out on this exciting adventure—reserve your spot today!

Energetica Exhibition

On loan from the NEMO Science Museum in Amsterdam, the Energetica exhibition is coming to the Community Science Museum. It’s a series of installations that allow visitors to experience the power of the elements as we harness them. From solar energy powering lighting, to ‘Wind Island’ that shows how turbines are able to use and control wind to create power.

Location: Community Science Museum – Special Exhibits Hall

Come see how the elements are powering our world, and learn about the future of sustainable energy!
